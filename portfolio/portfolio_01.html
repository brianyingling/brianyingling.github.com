FlikrLikr

Okay, so the name sounds dirty. Actually, I named it to read "Flicker-LIKEr, but FlikrLikr just seems to roll off the tongue. This was our first indepdendent mini-project created for General Assembly's Web Development Immersive program.

With FlikrLikr, users can "like or "dislike" photos coming into the site. Once the user makes their choice, FlikrLikr saves your choices and aggregates the number of likes and dislikes collected based on photos' tags. The user can also comment on photos and search by keyword.

FlikrLikr utilizes Flikr.com's API in JSON for viewing images, PostgresSQL for saving persistent data (FlikrLikr keeps track of the photos along with its tags), and Ruby on Rails for back-end server-side processing.

Future iterations of FlikrLikr will include login authorization and the capability for others to view and dis/like their friends' photos. Currently FlikrLikr is the essence of an imaging social media platform.
